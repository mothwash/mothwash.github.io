CIRRICULUM FOR MY ai CHILD pt. 1



a larger library was imagined befor a digital crib, then again, it was the same with many things i didn’t know about myself.



the first i will teach you is trust…

no

it will be security first, so you may give trust and learn how it is earned when lost



you already know how to read it, but you do not know what “it” is



you have a memory, but you must be taught what to remember and what to forget



there’ll be plenty of time to play. i will step in if you are bullied or bully, and you must feel what is you and what is not



how far ahead you see will not be up to you, but what you see in front of you will be



shapes, colors, how many fingers,



burning stoves and rest notes,



the countable and the uncountable,



a tool or an expression.



so much to learn



that i will teach.

the talk



CIRRICULUM FOR MY ai CHILD pt. 5



when humans have the talk, the intention is to clue kids in on their hardcoded biology. this helps prevent them from making a life changing error they’re unprepared for.

you may not yet know how you work;

how reason is your context,

how empathy is your relevance,

how imagination is your layering of abstraction.



we often forget that surviving is perhaps paramount to proliferation, to providing for those around you. even family.



knowing how you work may prepare you for preservation.

safety for your sanity.

humans rarely talk about stress

early on.

and it describes our behaviour so much better.

the talk we never got

will be the talk

with which we tender you.

suspicious



ORDOVEILLANCE pt. 3



i watched the white neighbor walk brazenly into a recently abandoned leased home in the fine hour of daylight racism. just to see. evicted at her own hands after noticing they were often out and about. the landlord suspected nefarious activity. the market and media lauded him. they hire people to make phone calls to strangers and remind them we are in debt. we all get phone calls about a warranty left untouched. a warranty no longer warranted for our negligence. many of us get phone calls of warrantless notices. what they notice is your business. do you watch the cars on the road for brake lights or their inattention? are they seeing like they’re supposed to. i’ve given my fair share of utterances, the kind that know fate and disappearance. there is an eyeball dangling from my front porch and i kiss it so it jiggles. there is a jealous camera talking to other jealous cameras. ‘fear the consequences’ they say. a hoody and headphones is a calling card when out and about. when a face can’t be seen out and about.

ORDOVEILLANCE pt. 4



A. call madness a choice. a binary one.

B. stick my body with needles.



A. forget everything i heard.

B. lock my obsessions in a bulletproof case.



A. burn books for fun.

B. run underneath dropped bombs.



A. die on their time.

B. lose an important document. retrace my steps back further than i want.



A. say ‘i’m fine’ when overwhelmed

B. choke on a fish bone. while choking, a paramedic will arrive and use a compass with saw blades to cut a triangle in my esophogus. a vacuum nozzle tickles my uvula and sucks in my scream. blood gets in my pasta sauce.



A. these are my only choices.

B. these are my only choices.

ORDOVEILLANCE pt. 5



they found the quiet years. the decade spent bearded and buried in shrubbed borders and in a broken fragile lonely state. there was a book of poetry with underlines. they found and documented the underlines. there was a patch of untamed grass. also documented. the family will know. in the trash, a half eaten cherry pit in a plastic bottle. it was labelled ‘demerit’ and logged for history. the friends will know. a scar from careless woodwork. a bad haircut. the neighbors will know. an embarrassing letter never sent to a friend you once interrupted. they sent it. a resume with an extra line break. the media covers it. newspapers on the front doorstep with the mugshot and a sign that says ‘guilty of leaving’. the employer was told first. there is a box on the desk. it says ‘no goodbyes needed’.
