Methodology

Core Pathway - Primary Assumption

This project was initiated with the idea that the following pathway would liberate labor. This pathway could be characterized by material gains for workers in the form of resources, time, and capital being used to organize divestment campaigns and to purchase or otherwise acquire our housing and workplaces collectively. With collective ownership and successful organizing to divest from systems of greed, the people could independently democratize the use of resources and institute policies that prevent greed.

Abstract

Liberating workers along the core pathway will require individuals to take action. The type of action varies across available resources, network, skills, and constraints. In the current state, listless activists, researchers, historians, and community organizers have suggested many different activities and actions that could be categorized along the core pathway. Individuals invested in liberating labor and combating greed are required to investigate and research local initiatives, weigh and consider their impact, and then assess the level of commitment they can make, measured against the constraints they face. Accurate conjecture and assessments of the present conditions could include large numbers of variables, the completed data for which is rarely available. It may also be difficult to align current local initiatives and skillset. Finally, objective analysis on how to prioritize time based on impact is largely excluded from mutual aid and collective action. This project aims to resolve these issues by compiling possible action, making instinctive assumptions, and building a flexible framework through which those assessments could be tested or amended. If the activities and constraints are measured and compared, then the required resources can be evaluated and shared. The first aim to test this hypothesis is to compile a list of activities and assess their scale, solvability, neglectedness, resource cost, needed skills, and time cost to finalize needed hours and resources. That sum total of hours and resources could then be used as constants when assessing how many people over what period of time and how much in resources would be needed to complete the activities. The second aim is to then build a tool that would assess the constraints, skills, resources, time, and network of an individual to propose suggested tasks or activities.

Task Generation

According to the primary assumption, tasks would need to be outlined that contribute to liberation. There would be high level tasks that broadly contribute to the goals and further specified subtasks to the high level tasks. Each high level tasks was categorized by which step in the path it supported, and then the high level tasks were scored for scale, solvability, neglectedness, time cost and resource cost. Additionally, nine broadly applicable capacities (or skills) were defined that were allocated into low, medium, and highly applicable categories.

Building the Framework

In order to assess individuals and recommend the tasks, there would need to be a questionnaire. The approach that was selected focuses on binary questions, often where the affirmative correlates directly to an impact on calculations. A question was written for each condition. The conditions were sorted by most impactful, and if answered in the affirmative, the constraint severity divided by the index minus five would be added to a running variable.





Then, there are ~10 questions each for time, resources, and capacities (or skills). For time, the score initiates at 5 and each affirmative adds half a point. For resources, the score initiates at 0 and each affirmative adds a full point. For skills, there are 11 questions, the score initiates at -1, and each affirmative increments a full point. The resulting skill score is divided by 2 for filtering purposes. For the skill score, each question translates directly to one of the capacities.





Finally, 3 network questions are asked to see if people in one’s network generally have more or less time, resources, and skills.

If the sum of the time score, resource score, and skill score * 2 are less than the condition score, the tool recommends that the constraints outweigh the pursuit of liberty and the tool recommends working to improve whichever scored lowest.

I would certainly welcome more rigorous approaches to test some of the assumptions made in this tool, but in order to protect the information collected, the standards of data maintenance and security would need to be severe.



I have some experience organizing across different cities in the US, but I would not categorize myself as a community organizer, solely because I lack experience compared to other, more avid organizers and activists. There is much experience talking with many different types of people and I feel confident in having heard their challenges. I believe in the freedom of all from greed, oppression, and abuse and want to do all I can to get us there. This product is a closer representation of the stories I’ve heard, my interpretation of what I’ve read, and making the most of my naturally-inclined cognition to take a concept and follow it through many different conditions.



If this disqualifies me from assembling this, I perfectly understand. The utility of this tool, until the methodology is refined and the assumptions tested, is relegated to the use of the reader’s judgement.

Intention

As this was a personal project, my intention was to assist with the internal assessments and prioritizing that I endured when considering how to support liberation. Additionally, I wanted to ease the access to recommended action that could be tailored by the experiences of different individuals. This tool is meant to offer self-reflection. I highly discourage anyone from answering these questions on behalf of someone else without explicit consent. I highly discourage anyone judge anyone based on their answers to these questions. I would actually advise against sharing what the tool recommends unless it’s something you’re considering pursuing.



Civil Disobedience

This tool does not include suggestions related to civil disobedience. That said, there is no doubt that civil disobedience is an effective tool for liberating masses. It has been effective historically and it remains effective in recent revolutions. It is important to acknowledge that not every action taken will allow others to proceed as usual. Some decisions will need to be made about who is welcome to proceed and who must be stopped to prevent further suffering or oppression.
